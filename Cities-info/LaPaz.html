Location: Nestled in a deep valley surrounded by mountains, La Paz is the highest capital city in the world, sitting at an altitude of approximately 3,650 meters (11,975 feet). Population: Around 800,000 residents, with over 1.5 million in the metropolitan area. Economy: Services: A major administrative and political center, contributing about 24% of Bolivia's GDP. Commerce: Bustling markets like the Witches' Market offer a glimpse into local traditions and crafts. Tourism: Attracts visitors with its rich history and stunning landscapes. Cultural Significance: Festivals: The Carnival of La Paz and the Alasita Festival showcase vibrant traditions and local crafts.
