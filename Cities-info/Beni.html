Beni

Beni is a tropical paradise with lush vegetation and a warm climate. Its rivers and wetlands provide a unique ecosystem, making it a great destination for eco-tourism.

Location: Situated in northeastern Bolivia, Beni is known for its extensive wetlands and rivers. Capital: Trinidad. Population: Approximately 445,964 residents. Economy: Agriculture: Major crops include rice, cassava, and fish farming. Tourism: Known for its natural beauty and wildlife. Cultural Significance: Rich in folklore and traditional music, Beni celebrates various festivals throughout the year. Natural Attractions: Madidi National Park: A biodiversity hotspot, home to countless species of plants and animals. Beni River: Vital for local transportation and fishing, offering stunning views.
